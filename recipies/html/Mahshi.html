Mahshi

Brief introduction:

Mahshy is a traditional Middle Eastern dish that consists of vegetables, such as eggplant, zucchini, and bell peppers, stuffed with a mixture of rice, herbs, and spices. The dish is then cooked in a tomato-based sauce and served hot. Mahshy is a popular dish in countries such as Egypt, Lebanon, and Syria, and is often served during special occasions and holidays.

The history of Mahshy can be traced back to the Ottoman Empire, where it was a popular dish among the ruling class. Over time, the dish spread throughout the Middle East and became a staple in many households. Today, Mahshy is enjoyed by people all over the world and has become a symbol of Middle Eastern cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Vegetables (6-8 such as eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, or grape leaves)
  • Rice (1 cup)
  • Herbs (1/2 cup such as parsley, dill, and mint)
  • Spices (1 tsp such as cumin, coriander, and paprika)
  • Tomato sauce (1 can)
  • Garlic (2 cloves)
  • Lemon juice (2 tbsp)
  • Olive oil (1/4 cup)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

Preparing Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare the vegetables by cutting off the tops and scooping out the insides.
  3. Rinse the rice and mix it with the herbs, spices, garlic, and lemon juice.
  4. Stuff the vegetables with the rice mixture and place them in a baking dish.
  5. Pour the tomato sauce over the vegetables and add enough water to cover them.
  6. Drizzle olive oil over the top and season with salt and pepper.
  7. Cover the dish with foil and bake for about 45 minutes to an hour, or until the vegetables are tender and the rice is cooked.
  8. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes to brown the top.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy!
